# Homepage & Site Architecture (evidence-based, 2024–2026)

## Governing facts (what the evidence forces)
1. Credibility judged in **~50 ms**; stay/leave decided in **~10 s**;
   value prop + identity + proof + primary CTA must be above the fold.
2. Users **scan, ~25% of text read**, F/Z pattern, <1 min visits → section
   *headlines* carry the message; load-bearing content top-left/high.
3. SMB/B2B buyers self-serve **~70-80%** before contact, ~95% research online,
   ~75% want a rep-free path → the site must let them self-qualify (work, who,
   scope, price) without talking to anyone.
4. Visitor↔brand gap (Orbit Media): buyers want **examples of work, what it
   costs, who you are**; brands over-index on brand story/abstract proof. The
   "proof + identity higher" instinct is evidence-backed.
5. **About is the #2-visited page** (~52% of first visits) — identity is a
   primary conversion asset, especially for a solo studio where the operator
   *is* the product.

## Canonical homepage section order (numbered, with rationale)
1. **Sticky header** — logo top-left (93%), 4-6 nav items, one distinct primary
   CTA top-right (contact/quote), language switcher top-right (NOT a nav item,
   no flag-only). Sticky nav ≈ +22% nav speed.
2. **Hero (above fold)** — outcome H1 = value prop; one-line subhead (who-for +
   differentiator); ONE primary CTA + ONE low-friction secondary (WhatsApp);
   embedded proof token (marquee result / logos / real-work shot). Clear over
   clever.
3. **Proof strip (right below hero)** — logos / rating / one-line result;
   testimonials below hero have tested ≈ +34%.
4. **The work / portfolio — top third, NOT buried.** 4-6 strong,
   vertically-filterable case cards; the work is the pitch. Single most common
   structural fix. A nav item too.
5. **Services** — concrete, outcome-framed, scannable; specific > umbrella;
   each block ends with a CTA.
6. **About / who does this — mid-page** — real photo, name, first-person story,
   credibility (years, niche, why solo = senior attention). A 1-line "built
   personally by X" also rides higher (hero/proof). Full story → `/about`.
7. **Process** — 3-4 plain steps; de-risks unfamiliar SMB buyers.
8. **Pricing / investment signal — present** ("Projects from €X" / ranges).
   Missing price is a top agency mistake and a self-qualification blocker.
9. **Testimonials** — detailed, photo + name + role + business; ALSO place one
   adjacent to the final CTA (peak-doubt). Photos ≈ +29%; near-CTA ≈ +6%.
10. **FAQ** — objection-killer (timeline, languages, what's included, why solo,
    ongoing support). Aids scanners + SEO.
11. **Final CTA band** — restate value + primary CTA + WhatsApp + a testimonial
    beside it.
12. **Footer as navigation** — full sitemap, contact, languages, social.

Rules: proof ≥4× (hero token, strip, portfolio, near final CTA); **every major
section ends with a CTA** (CTA-less sections are a named mistake); one primary
action repeated (multiple CTAs to the *same* destination good; competing
destinations −up to 266%); clicks-to-contact ≤1 from any scroll position.

## CTA strategy
One repeated primary action; specific verbs ("Get my quote" > "Submit"; never
"Click here"; specific/personalized CTAs ≈ +202%). Low-friction secondary =
WhatsApp click-to-chat with pre-filled message (≈2-tap funnel; ideal for
Spanish SMBs), persistent on mobile (sticky bottom bar) — never bury the
primary CTA in the hamburger. Forms ≤5 fields (≤5 ≈ doubles conversion;
11→4 = +160%).

## Multi-page vs one-page (recommendation)
**Hybrid.** Long, sequenced homepage = highest-converting primary path
(linear, no nav decisions). PLUS dedicated indexed pages for SEO/depth:
`/` (sequenced home) · `/work` + `/work/[project]` (filter by vertical) ·
`/services` or per-vertical landing pages (one intent/page = the SEO win) ·
`/about` (full operator story) · `/contact` · `/blog`. Homepage *previews*
each with links. One keyword/page beats one keyword/site.

## Trilingual (EN/ES/KA) IA
Identical nav/structure per language; switcher header top-right + footer,
explicit. URLs = subdirectories under one domain to consolidate authority;
bidirectional `hreflang` + `x-default` (75% of setups have errors; one error
voids the cluster — defer mechanics to seo-geo-audit). ES likely the
default/primary for a Spain lead goal.

## DO / AVOID
DO: value prop H1 + proof token + 1 primary CTA above fold; portfolio top
third; About mid + identity line high; price present; proof ≥4× incl. near
final CTA; real photos/names; 4-6 sticky nav items + contact CTA top-right;
footer = full nav; sticky mobile CTA + WhatsApp; ≤5 form fields; per-vertical
+ case-study pages.
AVOID: portfolio/identity buried low (the classic mistake); no pricing; vague
"we make websites"; hidden contact; testimonial carousels; stock over real
photos; CTA-less sections; competing CTAs; pure one-pager when SEO/inbound +
trilingual; language switcher in main nav or flag-only.
