# Conversion Copywriting + Human (non-AI) Voice

Operating principle: clarity beats cleverness; specificity beats adjectives;
the customer's words beat yours. Solo operator = first-person singular **"I",
never "we"** (owning "it's just me — you work with the person who builds it" is
a selling point). Every line must pass Harry Dry's three tests: **can I
visualize it? can I falsify it? could nobody else say it?** If a competitor's
site could run the sentence unchanged, cut it.

## AI-slop kill-list (detect → rewrite)
- **Words:** elevate, unlock, empower, seamless, robust, leverage, utilize,
  holistic, cutting-edge, best-in-class, synergy, streamline, reimagine,
  crafted, tailored/bespoke (when empty), "solutions", "digital
  landscape/age/world", "in today's…", "we are passionate about", "your trusted
  partner", "take it to the next level", "drive results".
- **Structures:** rule-of-three triads; "not just X, but Y" / "it's not…it's…";
  "-ing" tail clauses ("…helping businesses grow"); hollow verb-frames ("built
  to…", "designed to deliver"); hedging ("may help", "can potentially");
  em-dash overuse (≤1 per ~150 words; prefer periods).
- **Moves:** all-"we"/no-"you"; fake urgency/scarcity badges; vague adjective
  with no number; claims with no proof; sentences true of any company anywhere.
- **Three tests on every line:** read-aloud (would you say it to a client over
  coffee?); "so what?" (chase to a concrete outcome); "could a competitor paste
  this?" (if yes, add specifics until no).

## How to sound human (method)
**Message-mine before writing:** pull exact phrases from real reviews,
WhatsApp/sales notes, "why did you hire me?" answers; write headlines in *their*
words ("my old site looked like it was from 2010"). Replace adjectives with
concrete nouns/numbers ("better service" → "I reply the same day, in your
language"). Target Flesch grade 6-8, avg sentence <15-20 words, active voice;
vary sentence length for rhythm. Solo = "I".

## Headline / value-prop formulas
Outcome + who + mechanism, ranked by the 4 U's (Useful > Urgent > Unique >
Ultra-specific). Skeletons:
- "How to [outcome] without [pain]" — "Get a website that fills your
  appointment book — without paying an agency €8,000."
- "[Who] who [situation] get [outcome]" — "Dental clinics in Valencia get a
  site patients actually book from."
- Falsifiable + differentiated — "Most clinic sites load in 6 seconds. Yours
  will load in under 2 — the difference between a booking and a bounce."
No cleverness in H1 (NN/g: objective+concise+scannable copy tested +124%
usability vs marketese).

## CTA + form microcopy
First-person, outcome-based buttons (first-person/"my" CTAs ≈ +38-90%;
"Submit" underperforms 30-40%): ❌ "Submit"/"Learn more" → ✅ "Get my free
quote", "Book a 15-min call". Friction-reducer under button: "No obligation. I
reply within one business day, in EN/ES/KA." Labels always-visible top (never
placeholder-only). Errors state problem + fix ("This email is missing the @ —
try name@domain.com", not "Invalid"). Privacy reassurance by the email field.

## Trust & objection copy
Testimonials before→after→specific result, include the objection (reverse
testimonial): "I almost didn't hire a one-person studio — I worried about
reliability. [name] delivered in 3 weeks and our bookings doubled. — María,
[Clinic], Valencia." FAQ = objection-killer, not filler ("Why just one
person?", "What if I need changes later?", "Why not Wix myself?", "How much,
really?"). Pricing copy states a real starting number + what's included.
About (solo): specifics — years, niches, named results, why solo is better for
them; first-person, conversational, not a CV.

## Section-by-section (agency homepage)
1 Hero: promise (who+outcome) + real proof line + specific CTA; kill fake
badges. 2 Proof strip: named clients/cities + a real quote + a number. 3
Problem→outcome: mirror mined client pain in their words. 4 Work: each project
problem → what I built → measurable result. 5 Services: plain nouns + the
outcome each delivers; no triads. 6 Process: numbered, plain ("1. We talk. 2. I
design. 3. You review. 4. Launch."). 7 About: the human + why solo wins. 8
Pricing: real ranges + inclusions. 9 FAQ: the 6-8 real objections. 10 Final
CTA: repeat the hero CTA verbatim + reassurance line.

## Trilingual EN/ES/KA voice
Transcreate, don't translate — same plainspoken, first-person, specific voice
in each; localize idioms, currency (€), examples, proof. **ES = Spain register**
(formal "usted" for SMB owners; Spain vocabulary, not LatAm; warm-professional).
Rebuild the headline natively, don't word-for-word it. KA: same voice, short
sentences, check humor/concision survive.

## Microcopy for SEO/GEO
Lead key pages/answers with a declarative answer-first sentence:
`[Entity] is a [category] that [differentiator]` — "Onda is a one-person web
studio in Spain that builds fast, bookings-focused sites for local clinics,
restaurants and real-estate firms." Real Q&A FAQ; name entities + locations
naturally; high info-density (named entities + numbers per paragraph) beats
keyword density. No stuffing — plain declarative facts get cited by AI and read
human.

## BEFORE → AFTER (Onda)
- ❌ "Premium websites for businesses that need to look serious." →
  ✅ "Websites that get local clinics and restaurants more booked clients —
  designed and built by one person, not an agency."
- ❌ eyebrow "Quote in 24 hours" + "Taking 3 new projects for April" (fake
  urgency) → ✅ delete; replace with a real proof line.
- ❌ "We help businesses grow with stunning, results-driven web design." →
  ✅ "I'm Tymur. I rebuild dated SMB sites into fast ones that turn visitors
  into calls and bookings. Fixed price, ~3 weeks."
- ❌ button "Submit" → ✅ "Get my quote" + "I reply within a day, in EN/ES/KA."
