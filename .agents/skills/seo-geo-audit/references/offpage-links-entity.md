# Off-page SEO: Links, Digital PR, Brand & Entity (2024–2026)

Contents: 1 link value hierarchy · 2 what still works for a small operator ·
3 anchors/velocity/disavow · 4 brand mentions & entity (high priority) ·
5 why brand matters for SEO+GEO · 6 internal linking · 7 solo plan.

## 1. What makes a backlink valuable now
Order that matters: **editorial placement > topical relevance > authority/trust
> page that itself ranks/gets traffic > natural velocity**. Raw volume is dead.
Google link spam = links primarily to manipulate rankings; named spam: bought/
sold do-follow links, goods/services for links, excessive reciprocal, automated,
paid advertorials passing credit, large-scale guest-post campaigns, low-quality
directory/bookmark, footer/widget keyword links, forum/comment-signature.
Paid/guest links OK only with `rel="sponsored"`/`nofollow`. **Relevance beats
DR** (89% flag spammy outbound, 86% low-quality content as the red flags).
Referring-page traffic barely matters; **referring-domain diversity** + URL
Rating matter most. Links remain a baseline but are **not a top-3 factor** and
Google removed the "links used for relevancy" line (Mar 2024). Avoid PBNs,
bought links, paid guest networks, mass directories, exchanges, footer/widget
links, "DR boosting".

## 2. What still works for a solo operator (ranked by ROI)
1. **Reactive digital PR / expert commentary** — #1 tactic (48.6% of SEOs);
   data-led + expert quotes; respond to journalist queries (Featured/HARO,
   Qwoted, SourceBottle, ResponseSource, Spanish equivalents) within ~1 hr.
2. **"Built by [you]" portfolio backlinks** — one branded contextual link in
   each client site's footer/about; high-relevance, compounding; branded
   anchor, not keyword-stuffed.
3. **Local sponsorships & partnerships** — events, charities, clubs, suppliers,
   business associations; genuine and defensible.
4. **Linkable assets / data-led content** — a small original study, free tools,
   templates; statistics/citations are the backbone of digital PR.
5. **Curated directories only**: Google Business Profile, Bing Places, Clutch,
   DesignRush, Sortlist, The Manifest, G2, plus relevant national/local ones
   (Spain: Páginas Amarillas, chambers). Skip generic link directories.
Dead/diminishing: mass guest posting (85% of guest sites DR<40), generic
directories, exchanges, comment/forum links, spinning, "1000 backlinks" gigs.

## 3. Anchors, velocity, disavow
Healthy mix ≈ 40–50% branded, 20–30% partial/topical, 10–15% generic, **only
5–10% exact-match** (>1–5% exact-match patterns risk over-optimization;
exact-match correlates only weakly anyway). Let inbound anchors stay natural.
Velocity: earned-looking, matching PR events, no bulk spikes. **Disavow** is a
"billable waste of time" per Google unless you have an actual manual link-spam
action — "toxic links" is a vendor concept Google ignores. Do not run disavow
tools or chase third-party "toxic scores".

## 4. Brand mentions & the entity (strategic center of gravity)
**Brand mentions now rival/exceed links.** Ahrefs 75,000-brand study (Aug
2025): brand web mentions correlate **0.664** with AI-Overview visibility vs
**0.218 for backlinks (~3×)**; top-quartile-mention brands get ~10× more AI
citations. Bing ranks mentions-without-links by trust; ~81% of SEOs say
unlinked mentions affect rankings; higher branded search volume ↔ better AI
visibility. **Build the entity:** `Organization` schema with `sameAs` to every
owned profile (single highest-leverage entity signal); create/maintain
Crunchbase, LinkedIn company page, Clutch/Sortlist/DesignRush, GBP, **Wikidata**
item (verified Wikidata ≈ 3.2× Knowledge Panel, ≈2.7× AI-citation likelihood);
Wikipedia only if genuinely notable; iron-clad **NAP consistency** (40+
accurate citations ≈ 53% higher local rank; 20 consistent beat 100
inconsistent); reclaim unlinked mentions but value the mention itself.

## 5. Why brand/authority matters for both SEO and GEO
Classic SEO: links downgraded, brand/entity prominence + engagement up; aim
(mentions + citations) ÷ links > 1.0 (ratios <1 look unnaturally link-built).
GEO: ~82% of AI citations are earned media; multi-publication distribution up
to +325% citations; ~83% of AI-Overview citations are pages outside organic
top-10 (AI visibility partly independent of rank) yet AIO presence lifts
adjacent organic CTR ~35%. ChatGPT/Perplexity citation overlap ~11% — broad,
repeated, credible third-party presence (incl. Reddit/Quora/YouTube) is the
unifying lever. Recency matters (ChatGPT: 76% of top-cited pages updated within
30 days). GEO = the same motion as brand PR + freshness + stats/quotes/citations.

## 6. Internal linking (highest-ROI lever fully in your control)
~66% of pages have only one internal link — most sites waste this. Hub-and-
spoke: service/landing hubs link down to case studies/blog spokes, spokes link
back up; contextual in-body links > nav/footer; descriptive anchors; push
equity from strongest pages (home, top case study) to money/service pages and
new content; bi-directional internal linking ≈ 2.7× AI-citation probability.
Ensure every important page has ≥3 contextual internal links.

## 7. Realistic solo plan (~4–6 hrs/wk)
2–3 reactive PR/query responses/wk; "Built by" link on every new client site;
one local sponsorship/partnership per quarter; one data-led asset per quarter
then pitch it; keep Crunchbase/Clutch/LinkedIn/Wikidata/GBP + `sameAs` current
and NAP-consistent; monthly brand-mention scan + reclaim; continuous internal-
linking hygiene. Budget ≈ $24–60/mo PR tool + sponsorship spend. Mindset: few
relevant earned links + a loud, consistent, well-cited entity beats any volume
play, and is the only approach that compounds for both Google and AI.
