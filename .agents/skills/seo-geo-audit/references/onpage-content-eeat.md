# On-page SEO, Content, Keyword/Intent & E-E-A-T (2024–2026)

4 topical authority/funnel · 5 E-E-A-T · 6 helpful-content/AI/parasite ·

## 1. Keyword research & search intent
Four intents (informational, commercial, transactional, navigational) but
**classify by the live SERP, not by guessing**. For every target query, read
the top 5–10 results and extract the **3 C's**: Content *type* (blog vs
service/landing vs category), *format* (how-to, listicle, comparison, case
study), *angle* (year, price, "for [niche]", local). Match the dominant
pattern; intent mismatch ≈ won't rank. **Long-tail is the strategy for a small
site**: ~92% of keywords get ≤10 searches/mo but the aggregate dominates;
lower difficulty, higher conversion, disproportionately important under AI
Overviews. Target KD <50 for low-authority sites. **Local service keywords are
highest-ROI**: `[service] [city]`, `[city] [service]`, neighborhood/vertical
modifiers in customer language. One dedicated page per real service ×
location/vertical; keyword-map every URL (one primary + supporting terms).
Avoid a single "we serve everyone everywhere" page (doorway pattern).

## 2. Titles, meta, Open Graph
Optimize titles by **pixels** (~600px / ~50–60 chars), primary keyword + value
front-loaded, brand at end. Google rewrites ~76% of titles / ~62–70% of metas
when boilerplate/stale/mismatched/no clear H1. Unique title per page + matching
H1. Meta description: not a ranking factor, +10–30% CTR; unique 150–160 chars
with specifics + soft CTA. Open Graph: per-page `og:title`/`og:description`,
`og:image` **1200×630**, in static HTML (3–5× social CTR).

## 3. Heading hierarchy & skimmability
One H1 = page topic matching title intent; logical H2→H3 (don't skip).
Users scan (~20–28% of words read; F-pattern). Front-load keywords in
human-written headings; short paras, bullets, a descriptive subhead ~every 200
words; make each section a self-contained, snippet-able chunk (AI engines
retrieve passages). Semantic HTML (`article`/`nav`/`section`).

## 4. Topical authority / clusters / funnel
Pillar + cluster model, internally linked with descriptive anchors (≈+30%
organic, rankings hold ~2.5× longer). Start with 3–5 topics of genuine
first-hand expertise. Cover TOFU (educational), MOFU (case studies,
comparisons, process), BOFU (service/pricing/testimonials/contact). Depth =
fully satisfy intent vs competitors, not arbitrary word counts.

## 5. E-E-A-T (Trust is the anchor)
2024–2026: YMYL/non-YMYL line blurring — apply everywhere; **first-hand
Experience now rivals formal credentials**. Concrete, winnable for a small
site: named author/founder bylines + substantive author page; a **specific,
verifiable About page** (real name, location, story, credentials); **real
contact details** (address, phone, email — outperforms form-only); original
media (real screenshots, before/after, numbers); named testimonials (name +
photo + company + *specific* outcome — "increased bookings 38%" > "great to
work with"); process/methodology transparency.

## 6. Helpful-content / people-first / AI / parasite
Helpful Content System folded into core (Mar 2024) — signals not a separate
hammer; ~45% low-quality reduction. Apply Who/How/Why + self-assessment.
**Avoid**: mass coverage without expertise, value-free summarizing, writing to
word counts, fake freshness, AI-at-scale to manipulate. AI content judged by
the same bar — fine if expert-reviewed, accountable named author, original
insight; **scaled content abuse** penalizes volume-without-value regardless of
author. Site-reputation/parasite SEO (tightened Nov 2024, Aug 2025 spam):
don't host third-party content to exploit your domain.

## 7. Images
Best practice: descriptive filenames, specific non-stuffed alt (empty for
decorative), explicit width/height (CLS), `loading="lazy"` below-fold only
(never the LCP/hero), WebP/AVIF + `srcset`. **Critical: Google does not index
CSS `background-image`s.** Portfolio/proof visuals delivered only as CSS
backgrounds are invisible to Google Images and weaken page-topic signals —
render them as real `<img>`/`<picture>` with descriptive filename + alt;
reserve CSS backgrounds for decorative texture.

## 8. Freshness vs doorway pages
Update genuinely (data, examples, screenshots) where intent rewards freshness;
**never just change dates** (deceptive). Programmatic/location pages are fine
**only if each is unique, intent-specific, genuinely useful**; thin/templated/
near-duplicate = doorway (penalized, esp. Aug 2025 spam + SpamBrain). Per-
vertical/per-city/per-language pages must each carry unique copy, real
local/vertical examples, distinct proof — not a spun template.

## 9. Content that wins clients
Service pages: **problem → solution → proof → CTA**, benefits not features, one
clear primary CTA. **Case studies = strongest asset**: Problem → Solution →
Result with concrete metrics + client quote. Portfolio: real visuals (as
`<img>`), context, outcome. Testimonials: name + photo + company + specific
result. Put trust signals adjacent to every BOFU CTA. (Conversion mechanics &
numbers: measurement-updates-cro.md §3.)
