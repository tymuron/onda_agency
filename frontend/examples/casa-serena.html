Casa

A fourteen-room sanctuary on the Mediterranean coast, where golden light meets ancient stone and the pace of life slows to the rhythm of the tide.

Reserve Your Stay

"Where the Aegean meets ancient stone, time bends toward stillness -- and every morning tastes of salt, warm bread, and the slow unraveling of the day."

Casa Serena was born from the belief that the finest hotels do not compete for your attention. They quiet everything else. Fourteen rooms, each a dialogue between light, limestone, and the wide sea beyond.

We chose this coast not for its convenience but for its mornings -- the way the Aegean catches first light, the sound of cicadas through open shutters, the unhurried pace of a coastline that has not forgotten how to breathe.

Every surface here serves rest. Nothing is accidental. Nothing is excessive. What remains is precisely what matters.

01
The Spa

Rituals carved from the land

Our treatments draw on centuries of Mediterranean botanicals -- wild thyme, cold-pressed olive oil, sea salt harvested from the cove below. Two treatment rooms, a hammam, and a plunge pool set into the hillside. No playlist, no rush. Just the sound of water and stone.

02
The Table

A kitchen that speaks the season

Chef Katarina Pavlou sources within a 30-kilometer radius: line-caught bream from the harbor, tomatoes still warm from the vine, cheese aged in the caves above the village. Dinner is served in the courtyard at a single seating, under the fig tree, by candlelight.

03
The Gardens

Three terraces above the sea

Olive groves, wild lavender, jasmine that opens at dusk. The lower terrace ends at a stone wall overlooking the cove -- this is where guests read, sketch, or simply sit. No pool. The sea is twelve steps below.

04
Excursions

The coast beyond the hotel

A morning sail to the neighboring island with our captain, Nikos, who has fished these waters since he was fourteen. A walk through the hilltop monastery at golden hour. A wine tasting at the family vineyard two valleys over. We arrange everything. You carry nothing.
